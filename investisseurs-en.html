The model

Asset-light and digital-native: engineering, customer and data in-house; heavy capacity through equipped partners; each tier funds the next.

Four margin engines make it robust across the cycle. Recurring revenue — EnerClub™ and NRJ+™ loyalty, LPG deposit-and-refill at “Le Relais”, framework contracts with fleets, industry and institutions. The integration margin — one barrel, five margins, from field to pump to petrochemicals, consolidated in-house instead of leaking to intermediaries. A natural hedge — when crude is low the downstream breathes, when it is high the EOR barrel outperforms, while services follow activity rather than price. And a circular economy that turns costs into revenue — produced water (Water-to-Value™), sulphur, associated gas and waste — alongside asset-light income from EnerTech, EnerConseils and EnerTalents.

One barrel, five margins

The same barrel works five times — provided you own the chain.

MARGE 1
Extractionthe barrel that stays — well re-entry, EOR, early production
→
MARGE 2
Logisticsthe margin per kilometre — qualified trucking, relay depot-hubs
→
MARGE 3
Refiningthe crude-to-products spread — 500–2,000 b/d modular mini-refinery
→
MARGE 4
Distributionthe margin at the pump — Mobile Stations™, deposit LPG, B2B contracts
→
MARGE 5
Petrochemicalsthe molecule at the top — nitrogen fertilisers, methanol, sulphur

Today most of these margins leave the country or scatter among intermediaries. The EnerTchad model consolidates them on a single balance sheet: each link owned adds its margin to the same barrel — the crude is no longer sold just once.

The capital loop

Cash never sleeps: it climbs one tier per turn.

1Early cashoilfield services + downstream/distribution — trades that pay in months, not years
→
2Reinvested in barrelsmarginal well re-entry and EOR pilots — early production sells from day one
→
3Barrels transformedmini-refinery then petrochemicals — the molecule spread stacks on the barrel margin
→
4Stronger cash ↺consolidated margins reinvested next turn — more wells, more tiers, same discipline

That is what “each tier funds the next” means: no single mega-raise, but a modest capital base that turns — with early production as the loop’s starter motor.

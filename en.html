01 · Purpose

Fighting energy poverty in Chad.

Too many Chadians face expensive fuels, supply disruptions and entire regions left without reliable energy. EnerTchad’s purpose is to push back that precariousness — by producing, refining and distributing energy as close as possible to citizens, at the regulated (ARSAT) price, all the way to the last mile.

02 · The integrated chain

One company, the whole chain — from source rock to pump.

Three links form the backbone — Upstream produces the barrel, Midstream moves and stores it, Downstream transforms and distributes it to the citizen — extended by a chemicals arm, and irrigated by four cross-cutting capabilities.

Upstream · E&P
Mature-field revival, marginal blocks and enhanced recovery (EOR) with locally-sourced inputs — the Doba & Bongor basins.
Midstream · Logistics
Export corridor and distributed reserve: HSE trucking, pipeline integrity, depots — from depot to the last kilometre.
Downstream · Refining & retail
Modular, redeployable mini-refinery (500–2,000 bpd) and a target station network — fuel produced in-country, at the fair price.
Petrochemicals
The chemical extension of Downstream: fertilizers, methanol, LPG, sulphur, bitumen — plus EOR inputs and lubricants developed through technical partnerships for reliable, irreproachable quality.

Four cross-cutting support poles: Sustainability (GreenTech) · Technology (EnerTech) · Human capital (EnerTalents) · Advisory (EnerConseils).

03 · Why invest

The investment thesis, in three lines.

A market to reclaim
A country that imports its fuels: substituting imports with local production captures value that leaks abroad today.
Vertical integration
Each captured link keeps in Chad a margin currently ceded abroad — the engine of value creation.
Sequenced capital
From 10 M to 1 bn then 20 bn FCFA — a 100% Chadian build-up where each tier funds the next.

04 · Sustainability & communities

Energy sovereignty as a development shield.

Sustainability is built in by design: routine flaring avoided, gas-to-power on site, HSE-Q and compliance (OHADA, IFRS, EITI and ISO targeted). Local content first — Chadian jobs, training before extracting, and access to energy as a right, not a privilege.

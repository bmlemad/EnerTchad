Upstream · Oilfield services

Oilfield services & the capture fleet.

Integrated well services with a Chadian cost base — and a mobile fleet that captures, separates and sells the crude from the day the well comes on stream.

Our signature

The cost-reduction specialists — because we build local content.

A base of operations at the field’s doorstep, Chadian crews trained by our academy, inputs sourced in-country — from Kanem natron to cotton cellulose. Every localised link removes freight, hard currency and weeks of waiting: the same service, delivered at the field, is structurally cheaper — and the value stays in Chad.

Field signature · Zero spill, zero barrel lost

Frac-tank-style containers from the day the well comes on stream.

From well testing and clean-up onwards — before any permanent installations — the crude is captured in specialised mobile tanks set on dedicated containment, connected in series on the wellpad and movable by truck. Dual purpose: spill protection around operations and immediate commercial recovery — every test barrel is stored, metered and evacuated instead of being flared or lost. The well pays from the day it comes on stream.

The capture fleet — the right tank, at the right well, at the right time

Seven families of mobile, wheeled or towable equipment cover every moment of the well’s life, from the first test barrel to long-distance evacuation — all set on containment, connected in series, and gone again by road.

00 · Separation first

Mobile three-phase separator

The prerequisite for any capture: a containerised skid splits gas, crude and water at the wellhead — heater-treater for heavy crudes, metering at the outlet. Crude leaves on spec (BS&W < 0.5%) to the tanks, water goes to treatment, gas is measured instead of flared.

20-ft skid · 2,000–20,000 b/d — set by crane truck
01 · Primary storage

Towable 500 bbl frac tank

The wellpad workhorse: corrugated steel tank on skids with an integrated rear axle. Connected in series on containment, open top, heating coils optional for waxy crudes. Moved empty from well to well.

500 bbl · ≈ 79 m³ — towed empty (winch truck)
02 · Storage + road evacuation

Road-legal frac tank trailer

Same capacity, mounted as a multi-axle semi-trailer: it captures test crude for weeks on containment, then leaves directly by road towards the mini-refinery. Axle loads to be validated against track conditions.

500 bbl · ≈ 79 m³ — road semi-trailer
03 · Active recovery

130 bbl vacuum trailer

On-board vacuum pump: it draws directly from the well cellar, containment bottoms and test tanks, with no external pump. The zero-barrel-lost tool — drips, clean-up flowback, loaded fluids. Fast rotation.

130 bbl · ≈ 21 m³ — on-board vacuum pump
04 · Multi-well rotation

Hook-lift roll-off bin

A sealed hook bin, dropped and picked up in minutes by a standard hook-lift truck: one carrier serves several wells, and full bins settle on site. Made for marginal re-entered wells.

20–110 bbl — dropped/picked up in minutes
05 · Multimodal logistics

26 m³ ISO tank container

A stainless tank in a 20-foot frame: twist-locks, double valve, flame arrestors. Moves from road chassis to flatbed, even rail — the long-distance evacuation option towards the refinery or export.

≈ 165 bbl · 26 m³ — road, flatbed, rail
06 · First test barrels

Towable double-wall tank

A small tank with built-in containment on a light trailer, towable by pick-up and movable full at low speed on the pad. The buffer for the very first barrels of a well test, straight out of the test separator.

12–50 bbl · 2–8 m³ — towable by pick-up
Typical fleet · Wellpad at start-up

One mobile three-phase separator up front (the right crude on spec, water and gas diverted), two 500 bbl frac tanks connected in series on containment (primary storage), one 130 bbl vacuum trailer in rotation (active recovery and evacuation), two to three hook-lift bins for satellite wells, one double-wall tank for the initial testing phase.

Common specifications: heating coils (waxy crudes), vents with flame arrestors, reinforced ground clearance and sand tyres, foldable soft containments that travel with the equipment.

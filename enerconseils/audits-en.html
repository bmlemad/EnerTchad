Principle · Independence

Independence is not a slogan.

An audit is only worth its distance. At EnerConseils, the team that certifies an asset is never the one that advised on it: whoever verifies has nothing to sell on what they verify. Every finding is sourced — cadastre, production histories, regulations, field measurements — and put to contradiction before it is concluded. We write in a report only what we could verify; the rest is flagged as a caveat, not hidden.

Strict separationAdvisory and audit share neither the same team nor the same interest in a given asset.
Everything is sourcedEvery statement ties back to traceable data, backed by the sector Atlas and the field.
ContradictionThe audited party answers the findings before conclusion; gaps are documented, not erased.
Enforceable reportA dated document with explicit caveats that the client can present to a third party.
Offering · Four domains

Four audit domains.

Each domain answers a precise question, with enforceable deliverables and an identified audience. An audit can cover one domain or combine several.

Domain 01

Reserves & production

Independent reserves certification (1P / 2P / 3P), decline analysis, review of production curves and recovery rates, regional comparables. We separate what is proven from what is hoped for.

For whom

Investors (bankability, data room), the State (taxation & planning), operators who want credible figures.

Deliverables

  • Reserves certification report
  • Summary note & assumptions
  • Sensitivity set (price, decline, recovery)
Domain 02

Integrity & HSE

Asset integrity — pipelines, depots, stations: inspection, corrosion, cathodic protection, tightness tests. HSE & environmental compliance: produced water, flaring, discharges, ISO 14001 / 45001 frameworks.

For whom

Operators, insurers, the regulator and risk-conscious lenders.

Deliverables

  • Asset integrity report
  • Prioritised remediation plan
  • HSE & environmental compliance register
Domain 03

Technical due diligence

For an asset acquisition or an investor entry: data-room review, valuation of technical risks (reservoir, wells, surface), identification of red flags and value upside.

For whom

Asset buyers, incoming investors, lenders.

Deliverables

  • Technical due-diligence report
  • Risk & upside matrix
  • Reasoned go / no-go recommendation
Domain 04

Data, OPEX & compliance

Quality of histories and cadastre (geoscience, production); operational and cost audit (efficiency, OPEX benchmarks); regulatory compliance — OHADA, the hydrocarbons code, ARSAT, environment.

For whom

Operators, the State, finance and technical departments.

Deliverables

  • Data & cadastre diagnostic
  • Quantified OPEX optimisation plan
  • Regulatory compliance register
Method · How it works

An audit, in five stages.

From kickoff to follow-up, an audit engagement runs a short, documented sequence. The order never changes: we never conclude before the data and the audited party have spoken.

Stage 1

Scope & independence

The question and scope are set in writing. The audit team is formed at arm’s length from any advisory work on the same asset — separation is settled from the outset.

Stage 2

Collection & traceability

Cadastre, production histories, regulations, regional comparables, field measurements: every finding in the report will carry its source, backed by the sector Atlas.

Stage 3

Verification

Cross-checking data, sampling and field controls, independent tests. We verify what can be verified rather than take claims on trust.

Stage 4

Contradiction

Findings are put to the audited party before conclusion. Their responses are incorporated, remaining gaps are documented — a traced disagreement beats a façade of agreement.

Stage 5

Certified report & follow-up

A dated, enforceable report with explicit caveats, delivered with a decision summary. A follow-up checkpoint verifies what was actually fixed.

Framework · Standards

Audits anchored to recognised standards.

A finding is only worth as much as the language its reader speaks. Our audits rely on the frameworks investors, lenders and authorities expect — not an in-house method.

SPE-PRMSClassification of reserves and resources (1P/2P/3P, contingent): the standard investors and lenders read.
OHADA · SYSCOHADARegional accounting and legal framework: consistency of financial statements and governance.
ISO 14001 / 45001Environmental and occupational health & safety management.
API · ASMEIntegrity of pressure equipment, pipelines and storage tanks.
ISO 19011Audit conduct: evidence traceability, sampling, contradiction.
ARSAT & Chadian frameworkPrice, HSE and local-content compliance with national regulation.
Use cases · Triggers

The moment an independent audit becomes necessary.

You don’t commission an audit out of habit, but at a tipping point — when a decision commits capital or a signature. The most common situations:

Fundraising / equity entryA third party certifies reserves and the thesis before the investor commits.
Farm-in / farm-out / divestmentReciprocal technical due diligence on an asset changing hands.
Reserve-based lending (RBL)Lenders require an independent reserves evaluation before lending.
Annual review & reportingUpdating reserves and risks for stakeholders.
Litigation or expert reviewA sourced, enforceable finding in case of technical or contractual disagreement.
Compliance checkVerifying HSE, price and local-content alignment before inspection.

An enforceable file, ready for the data room.

An audit is judged by what it leaves behind. At close, you walk away with dated, signed and sourced documents — usable as-is before an investor, a lender or a partner.

Questions · Audit

What we’re asked most often.

The five questions that come up before commissioning an audit — short answers, no detour.

How long does an audit take?From a few weeks to a few months depending on scope and data access. The duration is scoped and written at the start, not discovered along the way.
Are your conclusions recognised internationally?Yes. We rely on the frameworks lenders and partners expect — SPE-PRMS for reserves, the OHADA framework for the financial side — so the report reads clearly outside Chad.
How do you guarantee confidentiality?Scope and access are contractualised before kickoff; documents stay in a controlled data room, and nothing leaves it without your agreement.
Can the team that audits also advise us?Not on the same asset. Advisory and audit are separate: whoever verifies has nothing to sell on what they verify.
What happens if a piece of data cannot be verified?It is flagged as a caveat, dated and explained — never hidden or presented as settled.

The model · designed by Chadian realities

Three horizons — each stage funds the next.

  1. Downstream + Services — from year one.

    A target network of 12 ultra-premium stations (hub-depots, "Le Relais" concept) extended nationwide by containerised Mobile Stations™ deployable in 24-48 h, plus asset-light oilfield services billed to operators already producing. Generates daily cash at regulated ARSAT prices — the same price in Mongo as in N’Djamena.

  2. EOR + marginal blocks — the growth engine.

    Chemical EOR with locally sourced inputs (natron, neem) targeting +8-17 % of OOIP on Doba's heavy crude, success-fee based: we are paid on additional barrels, the client's risk is capped. Then take over the blocks majors consider too small — our frugal cost structure makes them economic.

  3. Petrochemicals — 2030.

    Polypropylene (25,000 t/y target) and urea/NPK fertilisers from gas: agricultural sovereignty and the industrial transformation that anchors value in Chad.

Chad first. Absolute priority: cover 100 % of national demand before a single litre is exported. Neighbouring CEMAC markets and international sales take the surplus only.

Six signature innovations

Not an imported model — every field constraint became a design choice.

Mobile Station™

The station that comes to the customer: containerised, deployed in 24-48 h where fixed retail can't break even — and a distributed national strategic fuel reserve.

Modular mini-refineries

Standardised 500-2,000 b/d trains, prefabricated, catalogue parts, redeployable. Designed to run 24/7 under AI supervision: real-time yield optimisation, predictive maintenance, operational integrity.

Local-input EOR chemistry

ASP formulations from Chadian natron and neem — recovery chemistry without the import bill.

Water-to-Value™

Produced water (>85 % water cut at Doba) treated for EOR reinjection, salt recovery and irrigation — zero-discharge target.

USSD payments & EnerClub

Mobile-money native retail — no smartphone required. ARSAT-compliant loyalty credited in points.

Frugal robotics

Corridor drones, in-line inspection pigs, confined-space crawlers, automated EOR dosing — operated by Chadian teleoperators trained at EnerTalents.

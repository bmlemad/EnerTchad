Upstream · Enhanced oil recovery

The barrel that stays in the ground is the cheapest to find.

Conventional production leaves most of the oil behind. EnerTchad’s EOR programme targets it with chemistry formulated from Chadian inputs — measured, screened and piloted before any promise.

Why EOR

Conventional production leaves 65–80% of the oil in the ground.

Primary and secondary recovery typically extract only 20–35% of the oil in place (OOIP). On Doba’s heavy crudes (21–24° API), enhanced oil recovery is the difference between a closed field and a producing one — EnerTchad targets +8–17% of OOIP with chemical methods formulated from Chadian inputs.

Three families, three levers

Chemical · polymers & ASP

Unstick the trapped oil

Polymers thicken the injection water for a better sweep; alkali-surfactant-polymer (ASP) lowers interfacial tension to release capillary-trapped crude. The core route for Doba sandstones — the crude’s high natural acidity (TAN) even works in its favour, as injected alkali makes surfactant in situ.

Thermal · cyclic steam

Thin the viscous crude

Cyclic steam stimulation (CSS) heats the reservoir to make viscous crude flow. A card to play on the shallowest, most viscous pockets — depth is the limit, as steam loses heat on the way down.

Gas · WAG injection

Re-energise the reservoir

Water-alternating-gas injection swells and remobilises the oil. It waits for a gas source — one of the stakes of the sleeping Sédigui field.

The screening grid — measure first, choose second

Five reservoir measurements decide which method has earned the right to be tested: API gravity, viscosity, depth, temperature, permeability. Screening is a filter, not an oracle.

Sovereign EOR chemistry

Four Chadian raw materials, one sovereign chemistry.

Kanem natron for the alkali, neem for surfactants, cotton cellulose and gum arabic for polymers: the injection chemistry formulated from resources of the lake, the tree and the cotton field. Every localised input removes freight and hard currency from the cost of the additional barrel. Read: local EOR chemistry →

Downstream · Modular refining

A refinery that moves with the barrel.

Refine as close as possible to the market: a modular, movable mini-refinery — standardised 500–2,000 b/d skid-mounted trains, maintenance by module swap. The refining industry, at Chad’s scale.

Why modular

Where a mega-refinery is rigid and out of reach, ours moves with the barrel.

EnerTchad’s trump card downstream: a modern refinery that is modular, movable and deliberately small — standardised 500–2,000 b/d trains, prefabricated and skid-mounted, 100% catalogue parts. It deploys, redeploys and upgrades crude right next to the satellite blocks: a mobile asset, never stranded. (ambition — company in formation)

Six principles, one cost signature

01 · Modular

Capacity added brick by brick

Standardised trains assembled in the workshop, then connected on site. Capacity grows by modules, at the pace of real demand — investment is spread, never oversized. One exception to frugality: anti-corrosion steels for Doba’s acidic crude.

02 · Skid-mounted

No heavy civil works

The unit travels by road on its skid frame, sets down on light foundations and lifts off again without a permanent construction site. Capital is never frozen in concrete.

03 · Redeployable

Refining follows the crude

The unit moves towards satellite blocks and markets as production evolves — a mobile asset that keeps creating value instead of being locked to a single site.

04 · Right-sized

Small on purpose

Proximity refining at the wellhead scale: fuels produced in-country rather than exporting every barrel and importing every litre back. A frugal model built for Chad’s volumes.

05 · Module-swap maintenance

Replace, do not shut down

Availability first: a worn module is swapped — target under 24 hours — from a shared catalogue stock, instead of stopping the whole plant for weeks.

06 · AI-supervised & safe

HSE by design

Assisted operation and remote digital supervision (target), safety over throughput, and a sober hybrid gas-solar site energy supply.

The concept at a glance

Anatomy of the model: a short chain, on a skid

A modular mini-refinery is not a large refinery in miniature: it is a short chain, sized for local demand, designed to be assembled, operated — and moved. Here, at a single glance, is how Doba crude becomes fuels, gas and bitumen.

↔ Scroll the diagram horizontally.

Frugal capex, staged in tiers

Where a mega-refinery demands colossal capital in one go, the modular model invests train by train: you pay for capacity at the pace demand justifies. No oversizing, no asset frozen in concrete.

Atmospheric distillation, simple and robust

A proven process, few units, 100% catalogue parts: maintenance is done by swapping a module (target < 24 h) rather than a lengthy shutdown. Frugality is in the architecture — not in the critical steels, anti-corrosion for Doba’s acidic crude.

Anchored to the upstream

Crude arrives from nearby satellite blocks: a short chain, less transport, value captured close to the well. When a field declines, the unit lifts off and redeploys to the next.

Less crude exported, less fuel imported

Every barrel refined on site is a litre that does not leave for export only to return imported at a premium. The model tackles Chad’s paradox head-on: producing oil yet enduring expensive fuels.

How it works

From crude to product: the mini-refinery, step by step

A modular mini-refinery is not a large refinery in miniature: it is a short chain, sized for local demand, designed to be assembled, operated — and moved. Here is its journey.

Step 1

Receiving & preparation

Crude arrives from the nearby field, is metered, settled and stripped of its water and sediments: clean crude protects the whole chain downstream.

Step 2

Heating & distillation

Brought up to temperature, the crude rises in the atmospheric column: each cut — gas, gasolines, kerosene, diesel, residue — separates by boiling point.

Step 3

Cuts & specifications

Each cut is stabilised, blended and checked in the laboratory against national specifications: an off-spec product never leaves the site.

Step 4

Valorising the residue

The column bottoms are not waste: heavy fuel oil for industry and power plants, bitumen base for roads — every fraction finds its use.

Step 5

Storage & dispatch

Dedicated tanks per product, custody metering at loading, batch traceability: the mini-refinery supplies the Downstream depots, with the numbers to prove it.

Step 6

Redeploying the module

When local demand shifts or a field declines, the unit is dismantled and rebuilt elsewhere: capital follows the need — that is the whole point of “movable”.
